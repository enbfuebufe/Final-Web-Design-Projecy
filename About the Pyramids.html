ABOUT THE PYRAMIDS

The Pyramids of Giza, located near Cairo, Egypt, are ancient marvels built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, around 2580 to 2560 BCE, as tombs for the pharaohs. The complex includes three main pyramids: the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure. The Great Pyramid, the largest, stood as the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years. These monumental structures, made of millions of limestone blocks, showcase the advanced engineering skills of the ancient Egyptians. The Pyramids of Giza are a lasting symbol of Egypt's rich history and ingenuity, remaining the last of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World still largely intact.

The Pyramids of Giza

The Pyramids of Giza, located near Cairo, are among the most famous and iconic structures in the world. The Great Pyramid of Giza, built for Pharaoh Khufu, is the largest pyramid and was originally 481 feet (146.6 meters) tall. It remained the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years. The Step Pyramid of Djoser

Located in Saqqara, is the earliest pyramid in Egypt, constructed during the Third Dynasty. It was built for Pharaoh Djoser and is known for its unique step-like design, consisting of six stacked layers. This pyramid marks the transition from earlier mastaba tombs to more advanced pyramid structures.

The Pyramids of Abusir

Constructed during the Fifth Dynasty, are smaller than those of Giza but still significant. Notable pyramids at Abusir include those built for Pharaohs Sahure, Neferirkare, and Neferefre. These pyramids are less well-preserved but represent important steps in the development of pyramid construction during the Old Kingdom.

The Pyramids of the Queens

Smaller pyramids located near the main pyramids of the pharaohs. These pyramids were constructed for the queens and royal women of Egypt, often serving as their burial sites. Some notable examples include the pyramids of the queens at Giza and other pyramid complexes from various pharaohs, including those built for queens during the New Kingdom.
